Eastern Michigan University

...is located in Ypsilanti, a few miles MI north of I-94.

The 2013 Zone 7 meeting will take place starting on Friday March 15 at 7:00 PM and conclude around 3:00 PM on Saturday March 16. Please take a look at the agenda.

The majority of the meeting will take place on Saturday, including a student research poster session and several invited talks. Registration will take place at the EMU Rec/IM building on Friday evening. Activities that evening will include tours/night sky viewing at the Sherzer Observatory, short shows at the EMU planetarium, and various games at the EMU Rec/IM building.

On Saturday, the main part of the meeting will take place at the Halle Library on campus. We will walk/meet there after breakfast and the meeting will begin at 9:00 AM. After the morning session, lunch will be provided, and the meeting will close at approximately 3:00 PM. Following the closing, there will be a trebuchet launching contest at a nearby park for those that are interested in participating.

Lodging
As part of the meeting, lodging has been arranged for Friday night at the EMU Rec/IM building. Mats will be provided to sleep on, but those that choose to stay there will be required to bring their own bedding (don't forget a pillow!). Please indicate if you would like to use the Rec/IM building for your Friday night sleeping arrangements on the registration form.

Costs
Registration costs for the meeting are as follows:

  • $10: Participants who will not utilize EMU provided housing
  • $15: Participants who will use EMU provided housing
Dinner on the 15th and breakfast and lunch on the 16th are included in the fees and will be provided. Please plan on providing payment when you check in at the meeting.

Student Poster Competition
Posters will be judged by a group of physics faculty members and awards will be handed out at the meeting's closing. Please indicate if you would like to participate in the poster contest on your registration form.

Parking
Friday night: The gates to the EMU student center lot go up at 6:00PM. After that, you can park there for the night.

Saturday: All parking spaces are fair game (except spots marked reserved or metered spots). We suggest you park in the Oakwood Commuter Lot (Oakwood and Washtenaw Ave) as it is closest to the Library entrance.
