Rules/Restrictions

Following the Zone 7 meeting, we will relocate to a site a few minutes away from campus to have a trebuchet launching contest. We hope that many teams will participate! Below are the rules and regulations that have been set to ensure a fun and safe contest.

If you would like to participate, please email Justin Hodges directly.

Budget

  • The money put toward a trebuchet must amount to less than $100.00.
  • Donations count toward the $100 limit.
  • To verify this constraint has been observed, teams must submit their budget sheet.

Teams

  • A team may consist of any number of members!
  • Multiple teams from the same chapter may enter.
  • Participating students may only be in one team.
  • Members must be current students.
  • No faculty members may participate in design or construction of the trebuchet (They can advise but not design).

Projectiles

  • The trebuchets will be designed to launch cantaloupes (13-16 cm Dia).

Structure Guidelines

  • The trebuchet must not be taller than 2*Pi meters.
  • The base width must be less than Pi2/6 meters.

Release Mechanism

  • The person firing their team's trebuchet must be able to do so from a safe distance for release of about 3 meters.
  • Safe loading procedure must be observed.
  • The trebuchet must be at a stable static equilibrium without any additional constructs. (It may not require any additional mechanisms to hold it down, etc)

Launch Considerations

  • The trebuchet must be able to launch a projectile safely prior to the competition!

Counterweight

  • The trebuchet's counterweight must be securely attached to it.
  • The counterweight must be less than 250 lbs.
  • The trebuchet must be free standing.

Day of launch

  • Teams will be given one hour to set up their trebuchets.
  • Bring your own tools if your trebuchet requires final assembly.
  • Email Justin Hodges if you need any specific tools or power requirements that you are unable to bring.
